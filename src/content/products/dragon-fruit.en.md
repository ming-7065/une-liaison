## Dragon Fruit: Tropical Treasure Blooming at Night

Also known as "prickly pear cactus fruit," dragon fruit originated in Central America. In recent years, cultivation has expanded significantly in Taiwan's Pingtung and Nantou regions, with quality improving yearly, making it a rising star in export markets.

### The Allure of Red Flesh

Red-fleshed dragon fruit has flesh of a delicate purplish-red color, rich in anthocyanins and betacyanins with high antioxidant properties. The texture is delicate, moderately sweet without being cloying, and especially refreshing when chilled.

### The Secret of Cultivation

The unique characteristic of dragon fruit is its night-blooming flowers. The flowers only bloom at night, closing and wilting just a few hours later. Therefore, farmers must perform artificial pollination late at night to ensure successful fruit development.

### Health Benefits

Rich in Vitamin C, dietary fiber, iron, and Omega-3 fatty acids, it helps with intestinal health, skin maintenance, and cardiovascular protection. An ideal fruit choice for fitness and wellness enthusiasts.

### How to Enjoy

Cut in half and scoop with a spoon, or dice and add to salads and yogurt. Can also be made into dragon fruit smoothies, jelly, or used in savory dishes.
